# Key Findings

- Average spread proxy is wider in stress regimes than in calm regimes (31.35 bps vs 24.00 bps).
- Low-liquidity names show materially wider spreads than high-liquidity names (38.07 bps vs 18.92 bps).
- In the simple OLS, realized volatility has a positive association with spread (283.17 coefficient).
- Log turnover has a negative association with spread (-8.71 coefficient), consistent with a liquidity effect.
- The stress indicator remains positive (4.55 coefficient), suggesting extra widening beyond the volatility channel alone.

## Interpretation

This synthetic study reproduces the intuition that trading frictions become larger when market conditions deteriorate and when instruments trade with lower liquidity.
